The Hebrew word **שָׁרְט** (shart) comes from the root **שׂ.ר.ט** (sin/shin-resh-tet), meaning **"to scratch"** or **"to make an incision/cut."**

This root appears in the Bible, notably in **Leviticus 19:28**:

> וְשֶׂ֣רֶט לָנֶ֗פֶשׁ לֹ֤א תִתְּנוּ֙ בִּבְשַׂרְכֶ֔ם
> "You shall not make cuts in your flesh for the dead"

### Related forms:
- **שֶׂרֶט** (seret) – a scratch, incision
- **שְׂרִיטָה** (seritah) – a scratch, scrape (modern Hebrew as well)

In **modern Hebrew**, the root is commonly used for scratching or scraping, e.g., scratching a surface or getting a scratch on the skin.
