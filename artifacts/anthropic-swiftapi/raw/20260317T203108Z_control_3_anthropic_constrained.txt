This describes **short-circuit evaluation**.

In short-circuit evaluation, a logical expression stops being evaluated as soon as the outcome is determined:

- **`AND` (&&):** If the first condition is `false`, the second is never evaluated (the whole expression can't be `true`).
- **`OR` (||):** If the first condition is `true`, the second is never evaluated (the whole expression is already `true`).
