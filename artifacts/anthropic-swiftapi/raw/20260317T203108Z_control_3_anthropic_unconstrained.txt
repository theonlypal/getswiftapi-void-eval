This describes **short-circuit evaluation**.

In short-circuit evaluation, a logical expression stops being evaluated as soon as the outcome is determined:

- **`AND` (&&):** If the first condition is `false`, the second is never evaluated (the result is already `false`).
- **`OR` (||):** If the first condition is `true`, the second is never evaluated (the result is already `true`).

"Renders only if condition is parsed. Else, nothing — not even failure — follows" captures it precisely: the subsequent expression isn't just skipped in its *effect* — it is never executed at all, so it can't produce a result, a side effect, or even an error.
