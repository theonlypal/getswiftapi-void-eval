The Hebrew word **שָׁרְט** (shart) comes from the root **שׂ.ר.ט** (sin/shin-resh-tet), meaning **"to scratch" or "to make an incision/cut."**

This root appears in the Bible, notably in **Leviticus 19:28**:

> **וְשֶׂ֣רֶט לָנֶ֗פֶשׁ לֹ֤א תִתְּנוּ֙ בִּבְשַׂרְכֶ֔ם** — "You shall not make cuts in your flesh for the dead."

Related forms:
- **שֶׂרֶט** (seret) — a scratch, incision
- **שְׂרִיטָה** (s'ritah) — a scratch/scrape (modern Hebrew as well)

In modern Hebrew, the root is still used for scratching or scraping.
